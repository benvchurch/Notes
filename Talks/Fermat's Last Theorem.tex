\begin{theorem}
$x^n + y^n = z^n$ has no solutions in $\Z$ for $n > 2$. 
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Recall that the polynomial $f(z) = z^n + 1$ has the factorization,
\[ f(z) = \prod_{i = 0}^{n-1} (z + \zeta_n^i) \]
where $\zeta_n = e^{2 \pi i / n}$ is a primitive $n^{\text{th}}$-root of unity. Thus,
\[ x^n + y^n = \prod_{i = 0}^{n-1} (x + \zeta_n^i y) \]

\end{proof}
